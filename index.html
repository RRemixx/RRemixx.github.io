About

Current directions

My research sits at the intersection of statistical machine learning and real-world forecasting systems. I am interested in how predictive models can remain calibrated, useful, and interpretable when decisions depend on uncertainty rather than point forecasts alone.

Uncertainty quantification

Methods for reliable predictive sets, intervals, and decision-aware confidence estimates.

Conformal prediction

Online and adaptive conformal methods for non-stationary and multi-step forecasting problems.

Time series forecasting

Sequential prediction pipelines for public health, weather-related trajectories, and operational systems.

Causal reasoning

Trustworthy reasoning pipelines that connect structured assumptions with modern AI systems.
